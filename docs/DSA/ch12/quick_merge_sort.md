# Divide and Conquer Algorithms: Quicksort and Merge Sort

In the last chapter we looked at three examples of O(n<sup>2</sup>) sorting algorithms. These algorithms, while straightforward, were not scalable to large collections of data. This week, we consider the algorithmic technique of divide and conquer. Using divide and conquer, we'll define two more sorting algorithms that run in O(nlog(n)) time, quicksort and merge sort. 

## Divide and Conquer
Divide and conquer is an algorithm design strategy that focuses on breaking up a problem into smaller sub-problems until the solving the sub-problems becomes trivial. Then, the solved sub-problems are combined back into the entire solution.
